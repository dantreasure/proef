Leadership
Marieke van der Poel

Marieke van der Poel is Proef’s creative mastermind and founder. Starting with a strong fashion editorial and styling base in addition to studying Greek Philosophy, her experience and vision extends into macro trend research which supports and leads various cross industry translations. Marieke is highly regarded within the industry as being the rare individual capable of connecting high-level long term trending to time targeted and brand specific trend stories. Her proprietary approach allows her to craft concept, build brand strategy, and to create tangible and relevant product and design directions. 


Currently based in San Francisco, Marieke has previously lived in Italy and the Netherlands where she started her career in trend analysis, working as a stylist, editor and consultant for Italian, English and Dutch Magazines. She advised Peclersparis as a trend consultant for five years and worked with ESP Trendlab as a strategic partner.


Marieke has been featured in international media and presented at major trend and marketing events in the US including the National Retail Federation, Magic, Outdoor Retailer, the international gift show, Influx Insights. She is a frequent guest lecturer at various prominent art colleges around the country. She holds advanced degrees in both fashion editing (Milan, Italy) and humanities (Leiden, Netherlands). 


She believes in: just being, bringing beauty to the everyday, pushing conceptual and aesthetic visions, culture and kindness, dinners with friends, flowers, and having dogs in the office.


We believe in beauty, and we are always original.

Julie Yonehara

Julie Yonehara-1 is Proef’s creative lead and Partner. Starting with a background in industrial design and consumer electronics working for some of the worlds most recognized consultancies, she has branched into various fields of design and research including new material implementation, trend, CMF - color material and finish, and textile development. Julie is internationally recognized for her work which has pushed the aesthetic and conceptual boundaries across multiple industries including consumer electronics, soft goods, and various visual and trend strategies. Her work has been featured in various highly visible publications including Fast Company Magazine, Blueprint Magazine, and Swarovski Gem Visions, in addition to being included in a music video. 


Currently based in Oakland, Julie - a Hawaii native, has previously lived in London and New York. Moving from Hawaii to start a career in industrial design in the bay area, she moved to London to pursue her masters degree at Central Saint Martins studying Textile Futures. From there she has worked with Material ConneXion in NYC developing various material workshops and events. She has since then circled back to the bay area for warmer weather, and to work with various clients like Beats by dre and to become the creative lead at Proef. 


Julie believes in: Pushing conceptual weirdness and aesthetic, getting enough sleep, introversion, living with cats, a good pint, and the beneficial power of failure.

Sydel Sakuma

Sydel Sakuma-1 is Deputy-in-chief of strategy and investigation running Proefs LA office. She has a background in account planning, advertising, writing, marketing, and fashion. Her experience identifying and forecasting trends, social media, visual storytelling, and consumer analysis lends to her critical, inspirational, and creative thinking. She has inspired clients in the field of fashion, soft goods, consumer electronics, athletics and advertising. She also participated as the Chair of the communications committee of the Northern California Chapter of the Retail Design institute. 


A Hawaii native, Sydel currently resides in LA to be closer to the beach and remains as Proef’s main LA contact and creative resource. Sydel graduated from Chapman University with a major in Marketing, advertising, and public relations, and a minor in art. She received her masters in fine arts for strategy and account planning in creative advertising at the Academy of Arts University. 


She believes in: mermaids, unicorns, tchotchkes, cats wearing clothing, the healing power of kickboxing, speed-research, and beaches.
